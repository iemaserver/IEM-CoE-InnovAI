POST EVENT REPORT
IMPETUS: An Open Innovation Hackathon 2026
1. Event Overview
IMPETUS was a large-scale innovation-based hackathon successfully organized by Google 
Developer Groups On Campus, IEM (GDG On Campus IEM) in association with the Centre of 
Excellence for Innovation in AI.
This hackathon aimed to empower student innovators to build real-world problem-solving 
projects through technology, collaboration, and structured mentorship, while promoting 
innovation culture among young developers.
Additionally, IMPETUS was conducted as a part of the official campaign by Google Developer 
Groups India, strengthening its national impact and credibility.
2. Organizing Body
Organized by: Google Developer Groups On Campus IEM
In association with: Centre of Excellence for Innovation in AI
Institution: Institute of Engineering & Management (IEM) Kolkata
3. Event Duration
Start Date: 11th December 2025
End Date: 12th January 2026
This month-long hackathon included idea submissions, development phase, evaluation 
rounds, and the final offline event.
4. Participation Statistics
IMPETUS received massive engagement from student innovators across India and beyond:
Total Participants: 421
Total Teams: 236
The participation numbers highlight the strong reach and interest among students, making 
IMPETUS one of the most impactful events organized under GDG On Campus IEM.

6. Hackathon Highlights
Top 13 Finalists Selection
From 236 teams, the Top 13 teams were shortlisted after multiple evaluation stages, based 
on:
Innovation and uniqueness
Technical implementation
Problem relevance
Feasibility and scalability
UI/UX & presentation
These selected teams qualified for the Offline Final Round.
7. Offline Final Round
Venue
Science Auditorium, IEM Gurukul Campus
The offline round served as the grand finale of IMPETUS and marked the most engaging part 
of the hackathon where finalists presented their projects live before the judges and audience.
Judging Panel
The final round was evaluated by 4 Industry Expert Judges, who judged the teams on:
Innovation
Real-world impact
Technical depth
Product vision and execution
Business feasibility and presentation skills
Their insights and feedback encouraged participants and significantly improved the learning 
value of the event.
8. Benefits Provided to Participants
IMPETUS was designed not just as a competition but as a learning + growth + opportunity 
platform. The key benefits provided were:
1. Hands-on innovation exposure through real-world problem statements
2. Certificate of participation for all eligible participants
3. Certificates for Top Teams / Finalists / Winners
4. Industry-level project presentation experience for final round teams
5. Judges feedback & expert evaluation, improving project quality
6. Free Domain Benefits (.XYZ domains promo codes) helping participants in:
7. 
building their startup/project website
hosting portfolios
showcasing projects professionally
Nationwide visibility through GDG network, boosting student credibility
8. Community building opportunities, connecting students from multiple states
9. Confidence boost in teamwork and leadership, especially for first-time hackathon 
participants
9. Involvement of Google / GDG India

11. Conclusion
IMPETUS proved to be a high-impact hackathon that successfully motivated students to 
innovate, collaborate and build technology-driven solutions. With 421 participants, 236 
teams, national-level reach across 17 states, and participation even from Nigeria, the 
hackathon showcased the power of community-driven learning through GDG initiatives.
The offline final round at Science Auditorium, IEM Gurukul Campus, judged by 4 industry 
experts, marked a grand and inspiring end to the hackathon journey.
IMPETUS stands as a strong example of how student communities like GDG On Campus IEM, 
supported by faculty leadership and aligned with Google Developer Groups India, can create 
meaningful experiences that develop the next generation of developers and innovators.
